Q+A Host and Producer: Leeds Conservatoire 'Film Night'

During my time at university, I had the oppurtunity to produce and host monthly conversations with professional composers in a live-recorded Q&A show that was available on demand to students through our website.

The success of these recorded conversations was determined by my preparation -

  • researching the composers to generate compelling topics for a student audience
  • liaising with each guest in the weeks prior to the show to build a healthy rapport
  • promoting the show through word of mouth around the university

Taking on this oppurtunity gave me a deeper appreciation into the challenges of producing recorded audio - pushing me to consider how a producer needs to adapt to the technical limitations of the show along with finding the balance between generating material for a show that resonates with the target audiences and draws the most enthusiasm out of the guest speakers.

The Rest is Classified: LIVE - Podcast Liaison Assistant

In summer 2025, I had the oppurtunity to assist in the running of a live-recorded podcast at the South by Southwest Festival in Shoreditch, London.

I was heavily involved in the scheduling for the event - liaising with the podcast hosts to ensure the production timetable was clearly understood. I acted as their first point of contact for any queries or concerns that they had on the day and ensured their timely arrival to the venue before the recording began. With this chance to gain first-hand experience in this discipline of audio, I have gained more awareness of the importance of organised scheduling and have a better grasp on the time-sensitive needs of a successful show.

BBC Open Music Insight Day - January 2026
